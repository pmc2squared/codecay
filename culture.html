Culture

Code Cay is an island with rich and vibrant cultures. It has welcomed people from all over with its beauty and harmony, making it an island paradise to call home. The CodeCadians treat their island with great love and care, often rejoicing in its many blessings and abundance. This concept has been deeply rooted in its history and culture for many generations.

Welcome to Code Cay
Visitors are welcomed to the island with a dish of the island’s delicacy – briny and creamy Cayan oysters that seem to melt in your mouth.

Star of My Life
When a couple wants to make their relationship official, the guy presents their beloved with a starfish. The woman wears the starfish on a necklace or puts it in a bottle which she carries around with her to show the world that her heart belongs to a special someone.

Singing Bowl Massages
Code Cay is well known for its singing bowl massages which invoke a deep sense of relaxation. CodeCadians and visitors alike find that the rich blend of harmonic overtones which the bowls produce enhances their mental state of bliss and rejuvenation while they enjoy a massage with natural oils, salts and minerals.

Mermaid Parade
Code Cay is the only place in the world with mermaids, so the island often celebrates these magical creatures with parades on the beach during the summer months, where CodeCadians dress up like mermaids to frolic and dance the day and night away, often joined by their mermaid friends.

National Day in June
The country celebrates its birthday and its beauty. CodeCadians dress up in flowers or seashells in appreciation of the abundance of the island. In an island where nothing is wasted, CodeCadians spend the entire year collecting seashells from the beach or flowers from their gardens to create masterpieces to wear on this special day.

Singles Day in March
Code Cay has its own holiday where being single is celebrated. On this day, individuals can recognize their single friends, family members, colleagues and fellow citizens by giving gifts and flowers. There are multiple celebrations throughout the island where fireworks cap off the evening, and where singles can meet and mingle with other singles. Sparklers are lit during these celebrations to symbolize the spark within each individual, although in more modern times, blessings are given (usually by anxious parents) over the sparklers to help singles find their special someone.

Rainbow Rave in October
CodeCadians throw rainbow-hued powder at their neighbors during this festival, leaving everyone coated in color by the end of the day. The rainbow symbolizes the multiculturalism of Code Cay as well as the defeat of evil. The mermaids and unicorns who were the first inhabitants of Code Cay lived in peace and prosperity until the evil Nim took power by force. The mermaids and unicorns managed to overthrow him after many long and costly battles, and the unicorn leader Bright sent forth a rainbow to commemorate their victory and to remember all the lives that had been lost.
